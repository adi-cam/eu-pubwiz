Data in Publications About

A practical guide to user-friendly data in publications

This guide helps you with all data-related aspects of your publication: writing about data, creating data visualisations, and preparing and publishing data. The guide provides you with tips and resources that will make the data of your publication more accessible and reusable.

Preparing data for publishing

When public bodies share data openly, they increase transparency, accountability and value creation. Open data is data that anyone can access, use, modify and share for any purpose. In the case of the European Commission, documents and accompanying data are open data by default. Furthermore, whenever possible, they should be made available in machine-readable formats. The recommendations in this section guide you through publishing open data under the FAIR principles: making your data findable, accessible, interoperable and reusable.

Publishing the data and the data visualisations with your publication

The easiest way to publish your data is with the Publications Office of the European Union (OP). Through that publishing process you receive a unique identifier for your data and the data is included in the data.europa.eu catalogue. If you are publishing in another catalogue, you can still make use of OP services. You can consult PubliCare – the OP services catalogue – and request the services you need using Dempub.
